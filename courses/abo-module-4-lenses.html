Lens Materials

CR-39 (1.498): standard plastic, good optics. Polycarbonate (1.586): impact-resistant, thin, UV blocking, required for children/safety. Trivex (1.53): impact-resistant, better optics than poly. High-index (1.60-1.74): thinnest for strong Rx.

Key InsightPolycarbonate required by FDA for safety eyewear. Recommended for all children under 18.

Lens Designs

Single vision: one power. Bifocal: distance + near. Trifocal: distance + intermediate + near. Progressive: gradual change, no lines, requires precise fitting.

Key InsightProgressive minimum fitting height: 14-18mm depending on design. Measure to fitting cross.

Lens Coatings

AR: reduces reflections. Scratch-resistant: surface protection. UV: blocks harmful rays (built into poly/Trivex). Blue light: digital eye strain. Hydrophobic: repels water/oil.

Key InsightAR coating essential for high-index lenses - they reflect more light without it.

Multifocal Types

Flat-top 28 (FT-28): most common bifocal, 28mm wide segment. Round seg: smaller. Executive: full width. Progressive (PAL): no-line with corridor of intermediate power.

Key InsightSeg height measured from bottom of lens to top of segment (bifocal) or fitting cross (progressive).

Specialty Lenses

Photochromic: darkens in UV. Polarized: reduces glare from horizontal surfaces. Computer/occupational: optimized for specific distances. Myopia control: slows progression in children.

Key InsightPhotochromic may not darken in cars - windshields block UV that activates the lens.
