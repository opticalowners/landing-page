Module 3: Follow-Up Systems

Build systematic follow-up processes that keep patients connected and returning.

Why Follow-Up Matters

The space between visits is where retention is won or lost. Without systematic follow-up, patients forget about you.

Out of Sight, Out of Mind

  • Average patient visits optical once every 2 years
  • That's 730 days of potential competitors contacting them
  • Without touchpoints, your practice becomes forgettable
Key InsightPatients don't leave because they're unhappy—they leave because they forget about you.
Pro Tip: Think of follow-up as relationship maintenance, not marketing.

Recall Systems

A recall system ensures no patient falls through the cracks.

Recall Components

  • Database: Clean, updated patient records
  • Scheduling: Track when each patient is due
  • Outreach: Multiple touches at strategic intervals
  • Tracking: Monitor who responds
Key Insight3-5 touches are needed to get patients to schedule. One reminder isn't enough.

Good Recall Rate

Industry benchmark: 60-70% recall rate is good.

Pro Tip: Include insurance benefit reminders in recalls. "Use them or lose them!" is effective.

Communication Timing

When you communicate matters as much as what you say.

Key Timing Moments

  • Within 24 hours: Thank you message after visit
  • 1 week post-pickup: Check-in on new glasses
  • Birthday: Personal birthday message
  • Insurance renewal: Benefits reminder
  • Annual exam due: Recall sequence
Key InsightThe 24-hour thank you is critical—it reinforces their decision while experience is fresh.

Day/Time Optimization

  • Email: Tuesday-Thursday, 10am or 2pm best
  • Text: Tuesday-Thursday afternoon
  • Phone: Mid-morning or mid-afternoon
Pro Tip: Send birthday messages on the actual day, not a week before. Same-day feels more personal.

Channel Selection

Different channels work better for different messages and patients.

Channel Strengths

  • Email: Detailed info, links, newsletters
  • Text/SMS: Quick reminders, confirmations
  • Phone: Complex issues, overdue patients
  • Mail: Formal communications, older patients
Key InsightText messages have 98% open rates vs 20% for email. For urgent reminders, text wins.

Matching Channel to Patient

  • Younger: Prefer text and email
  • Older: Often prefer phone and mail
  • Always ask patient preference
Pro Tip: Always include an easy scheduling link in digital communications.

Automation

Automation ensures follow-up happens consistently without relying on staff remembering.

What to Automate

  • Appointment reminders: 48hrs, 24hrs, day-of
  • Post-visit thank you: Triggered by checkout
  • Review requests: 3-7 days after visit
  • Recall reminders: Based on last exam date
  • Birthday messages: Scheduled annually
Key InsightAutomate the routine so staff can focus on the personal. Automate the predictable, personalize the unexpected.
Pro Tip: Automated messages should still sound human. Read them out loud—if they sound robotic, rewrite them.
