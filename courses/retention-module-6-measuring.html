Tracking Systems

You need systems to capture and report on your metrics consistently.

Tracking Essentials

  • PMS Reports: Most systems have built-in retention reports
  • Dashboard: Visual display of key metrics
  • Review Frequency: Monthly minimum, weekly ideal
  • Trend Analysis: Compare month-over-month, year-over-year
Key InsightIf you can't easily pull your retention rate, you have a tracking problem that needs to be fixed first.
Pro Tip: Create a simple monthly retention scorecard with your 3-5 key metrics.

Benchmarking

Know what good looks like so you can set meaningful goals.

Industry Benchmarks

  • Retention Rate: 60-70% is good, 75%+ is excellent
  • Recall Rate: 60-70% response rate
  • Referral Rate: 20-30% of new patients from referrals
  • NPS Score: 50+ is good, 70+ is excellent
Key InsightCompete against yourself first. Improve YOUR metrics month over month before comparing to industry.
Pro Tip: Set goals 5-10% above your current baseline. Stretch but achievable.

Creating Improvement Plans

Turn metrics into action with structured improvement plans.

Improvement Framework

  • Identify: Which metric needs most improvement?
  • Diagnose: Why is it underperforming?
  • Plan: What specific actions will improve it?
  • Execute: Implement changes consistently
  • Review: Did it work? Adjust as needed
Key InsightFocus on ONE metric at a time. Trying to improve everything at once improves nothing.
Pro Tip: Run 90-day improvement sprints. Long enough to see results, short enough to stay focused.

Team Accountability

Retention is everyone's job. Create accountability across your team.

Building Accountability

  • Share metrics: Make data visible to all staff
  • Assign ownership: Someone owns each metric
  • Regular reviews: Weekly or monthly team discussions
  • Celebrate wins: Recognize improvement publicly
  • Address gaps: Coaching for underperformance
Key InsightWhat gets shared gets done. When the team sees metrics, they care about metrics.
Pro Tip: Post a simple retention scoreboard in the break room. Public visibility drives engagement.
